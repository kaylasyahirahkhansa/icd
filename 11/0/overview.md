## Hypertensive Diseases

[Hypertensive diseases](/11/1/1.md)

### Description
Although a continuous association exists between higher blood pressure (BP) and increased cardiovascular disease risk, it is useful to categorize BP levels for clinical and public health decision making.

### Exclusions
- Oedema, proteinuria, or hypertensive disorders in pregnancy, childbirth, or the puerperium (JA20–JA2Z)
- White coat hypertension (MC80.00)
- Involving coronary vessels (BA40–BA6Z)
- Pulmonary hypertension (BB01)

### Exclusions from Above Levels
- Certain conditions originating in the perinatal period (19)
- Certain infectious or parasitic diseases (01)
- Complications of pregnancy, childbirth and the puerperium (18)
- Congenital malformations, deformations and chromosomal abnormalities (20)
- Endocrine, nutritional or metabolic diseases (05)
- Injury, poisoning or certain other consequences of external causes (22)

### Coded Elsewhere
- Neonatal hypertension (KB45)

### Related Categories in Maternal Chapter
- Diseases of the circulatory system complicating pregnancy, childbirth or the puerperium / Hypertensive diseases (JB64.4/)
